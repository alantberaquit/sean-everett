Study: 3 Types of Proportion (Grade 6)

Quick Guide
1) Direct Proportion

As one quantity increases, the other also increases at a constant rate (or both decrease together).

  • Formula idea: y = kx (k is constant)
  • Example: More apples bought → higher total price.
Same direction
2) Inverse Proportion

As one quantity increases, the other decreases such that the product stays constant.

  • Formula idea: y = k / x
  • Example: More workers → less time to finish the same job.
Opposite direction
3) Partitive Proportion

Dividing a whole amount into parts according to a given ratio.

  • Idea: Split ₱600 among Ana:Ben:Cal = 2:3:5 → parts sum = 10 → Ana gets 2/10 of 600, etc.
Sharing by ratio

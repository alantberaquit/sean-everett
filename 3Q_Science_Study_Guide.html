1) The Big Idea must-memorize

Vertebrates = may backbone / spine (vertebral column) Invertebrates = walang backbone / spine
  • Vertebrates usually have an internal skeleton (endoskeleton).
  • Invertebrates may have an outer skeleton (exoskeleton) or a soft body.
  • Both groups need food, water, and oxygen, but they can have different body coverings and ways of movement.
Memory Tip: Vertebrate = ‚ÄúV‚Äù = Vertebral column (spine). If no spine ‚Üí invertebrate.
Vertebrates: fish, frog, snake, bird, cat, human
Invertebrates: ant, spider, crab, snail, earthworm, jellyfish, sea star

6) Essay Guide (10 pts) 5‚Äì7 sentences

Essay Template (copy this pattern):
  1. Define vertebrates.
  2. Give a vertebrate feature (spine + endoskeleton).
  3. Give 2 examples of vertebrates.
  4. Define invertebrates.
  5. Give an invertebrate feature (no spine + exoskeleton/soft body).
  6. Give 2 examples of invertebrates.
  7. Conclude with why classification is useful.
Vertebrates are animals that have a backbone or spine. They usually have an internal skeleton called an endoskeleton. Examples of vertebrates are fish and mammals such as a dog. Invertebrates are animals that do not have a backbone. Many invertebrates have an exoskeleton like ants and crabs, while others have soft bodies like snails. Examples of invertebrates include ants, earthworms, and sea stars. Grouping animals helps us identify them by their body structures.

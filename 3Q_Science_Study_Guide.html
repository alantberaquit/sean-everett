Science Study Guide

Grade 6 • Quarter 3 • Vertebrates & Invertebrates

BIG IDEA

Animals are classified into two big groups:

  • Vertebrates – animals WITH a backbone (spine)
  • Invertebrates – animals WITHOUT a backbone
The backbone (also called the spine or vertebral column) supports the body and protects the spinal cord.

VERTEBRATES

What are vertebrates?

Vertebrates are animals that have a backbone. They usually have an internal skeleton called an endoskeleton.

1. Fish

Water
Gills
Scales

Fish live in water and breathe using gills. Most have scales and fins.

Examples: bangus, tilapia, tuna

2. Amphibians

Land & Water
Moist skin

Amphibians can live on land and in water. They usually change form as they grow.

Example: frog

3. Reptiles

Dry scales
Lungs

Reptiles have dry, scaly skin and usually live on land.

Examples: snake, lizard, crocodile

4. Birds

Feathers
Wings
Eggs

Birds have feathers and wings. They lay eggs.

Examples: chicken, eagle, sparrow

5. Mammals

Fur / Hair
Milk

Mammals have hair or fur. Mother mammals feed their babies with milk.

Examples: dog, cat, human

INVERTEBRATES

What are invertebrates?

Invertebrates are animals without a backbone. Some have a hard outer covering called an exoskeleton.

1. Arthropods

Jointed legs
Exoskeleton

Arthropods have jointed legs and a hard outer body.

Examples: ant, spider, crab

2. Mollusks

Soft body
Shell (usually)

Mollusks have soft bodies. Many have shells for protection.

Examples: snail, clam, squid

3. Annelids

Segmented body

Annelids are worms with bodies divided into segments.

Example: earthworm

4. Echinoderms

Spiny skin
Sea animals

Echinoderms live in the sea and often have spiny skin.

Examples: sea star, sea urchin

5. Cnidarians

Stinging cells

Cnidarians have stinging cells used to catch food.

Examples: jellyfish, coral

QUICK EXAM REVIEW

With backbone → Vertebrate
No backbone → Invertebrate
6 legs → Insect
8 legs → Arachnid
Soft + shell → Mollusk
Segmented worm → Annelid
